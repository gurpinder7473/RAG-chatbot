Introduction to Artificial Intelligence
=======================================

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the simulation of human intelligence processes by computer systems.
These processes include learning, reasoning, and self-correction.

Key Branches of AI
------------------

1. Machine Learning (ML)
   - A subset of AI that enables systems to learn from data without being explicitly programmed.
   - Types: Supervised Learning, Unsupervised Learning, Reinforcement Learning.

2. Deep Learning
   - A subset of ML using neural networks with many layers.
   - Used in image recognition, speech processing, and NLP.

3. Natural Language Processing (NLP)
   - Enables computers to understand and generate human language.
   - Examples: chatbots, translation, sentiment analysis.

4. Computer Vision
   - Enables machines to interpret visual information from images and videos.
   - Used in self-driving cars, medical imaging, facial recognition.

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)
-------------------------------------

RAG is an AI framework that combines retrieval of relevant documents with text generation.
Instead of relying only on training data, the model retrieves context from external knowledge bases.
This reduces hallucinations and keeps answers grounded in real, up-to-date information.

Steps in RAG:
1. Document ingestion and chunking
2. Embedding generation (converting text to vectors)
3. Vector similarity search for relevant chunks
4. Injecting retrieved context into the LLM prompt
5. Generating a grounded, cited response

Applications of AI
------------------

- Healthcare: disease diagnosis, drug discovery
- Finance: fraud detection, algorithmic trading
- Education: personalized learning, automated grading
- Agriculture: crop disease detection, yield prediction
- Transportation: autonomous vehicles, route optimization

Ethical Concerns
----------------

- Bias in AI models
- Privacy and surveillance
- Job displacement
- Lack of transparency (black box problem)
- Misuse for deepfakes or misinformation
